# arXiv 2605.03072 - Tactical Wireless Network Design

Performs sensitivity analysis of tactical wireless network topology design under realistic operational constraints.

## Summary

Tactical military wireless networks have stringent reliability and connectivity requirements that differ substantially from commercial network design problems. This paper investigates how design choices — hub selection criteria, antenna beamwidth, transmission parameters, and structural topology rules — interact to affect network quality under realistic operational constraints.

The sensitivity analysis reveals which parameters most strongly influence network quality outcomes, providing guidance for network planners who must make design decisions under uncertainty. Hub selection criteria and antenna beamwidth are found to have particularly significant impacts on the coverage-connectivity trade-off, a central tension in tactical network design.

The work highlights that theoretical network design approaches developed without operational constraints often produce solutions that fail in practice. Realistic modeling of terrain, mobility, and operational requirements requires problem-specific approaches that differ from general-purpose network optimization.

## Key Arguments

- Network topology is highly sensitive to hub selection criteria in tactical environments
- Antenna beamwidth significantly impacts the coverage-connectivity trade-off
- Modeling parameters embedded in the objective function materially affect optimization outcomes
- Realistic operational constraints require approaches distinct from theoretical network design
- Sensitivity analysis guides decision-making under parameter uncertainty
